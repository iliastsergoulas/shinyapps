Agricultural land: Temporal and geographic analysis

Ilias Tsergoulas - www.agristats.eu

This is a report regarding:

  1. rural land in Greece,

  2. comparison with EU countries and the rest of the world,

  3. temporal changes based on Corine data from 2000 to 2012 based on processed data by Corine programs and QGIS.

Country analysis

Land has been the most important element of agriculture since man learned to harness the earth and its animals. Nowadays, despite new technology and innovative ideas (such as vertical farming, urban agriculture, precision agriculture), land is even more important given the famine-struck countries, the increasing population and the impact of environmental pollution on our food.

Greece has a large tradition in agriculture and production of high-quality products such as olive oil, dairy, fruits, wine, fresh vegetables, herbal plants and many others. Although Greece’s land consists largely of island and mountain areas, it has a high ratio of agricultural land (as much as 63,42%, as depicted on the following world map based on World Bank data).
